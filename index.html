When Does It Enter the Public Domain?

Calculate when any work enters the public domain under US copyright law. This tool replaces outdated Flash-based calculators with a modern, mobile-friendly interface.

üìö Understanding Work Types

üìñ Published Works

What it means: The work was distributed to the public by sale, rental, lending, or other transfer.

Examples:

  • Books sold in stores or online
  • Magazines distributed to subscribers
  • Sheet music sold to the public
  • Photographs in published newspapers
  • Movies released in theaters
  • Content posted online publicly

Key point: Limited public display (like showing a painting in a gallery) is NOT publication.

üìù Unpublished Works

What it means: The work was created but never distributed to the public.

Examples:

  • Personal letters and diaries
  • Unpublished manuscripts
  • Private photographs in family albums
  • Speeches that were only given live
  • Academic papers never submitted for publication
  • Artwork only shown in private settings

Key point: Copyright for unpublished works is usually based on the author's death, not creation date.

üéµ Sound Recordings

What it means: The actual recorded performance, separate from the underlying musical composition.

Examples:

  • A specific recording of "Happy Birthday" (vs. the song itself)
  • Podcast episodes
  • Recorded speeches or interviews
  • Live concert recordings
  • Audiobook narrations

Key point: Sound recordings have different rules than the songs/compositions they contain.

üåç Foreign Works

What it means: Works first published outside the United States.

Examples:

  • Books first published in the UK
  • Films first released in France
  • Artworks first exhibited in Japan
  • Music first released in another country

Key point: Foreign works may follow different copyright rules based on international treaties.

‚ùì Still Not Sure?

Q: What if a work was published in multiple countries?
A: Use the country where it was FIRST published.
Q: What about works posted online?
A: Posting online publicly generally counts as "publication" - choose "Published Works."
Q: What if I'm checking a song vs. a recording of that song?
A: These have separate copyrights! The composition (sheet music) is usually "Published Works," the recording is "Sound Recordings."
Q: What about works with no copyright notice?
A: For works published before 1989, lack of copyright notice may affect status. The calculator will help determine this.

About Accuracy

This calculator implements the duration rules found in 17 U.S.C. ¬ß¬ß 302-305 and is based on Cornell University's authoritative copyright duration chart. However, copyright law is complex and this tool provides general guidance only.
